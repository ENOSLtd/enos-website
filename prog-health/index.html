The Diagnostic

The 6-Cog Model

Six interconnected dimensions that determine whether a programme delivers - or stalls. Every one of them matters. When one fails, the others invariably follow.

COG 01

Strategy

Is the programme solving the right problem? We test alignment between strategic intent and what the programme is delivering, including what it has quietly dropped.

COG 02

Prioritisation

Are decisions being made on evidence, or politics? We assess whether the right work is in flight, the right work is being protected, and the right work has been deferred.

COG 03

Delivery

Are the mechanics sound? Velocity, dependency management, resourcing, risk tracking - we go beneath the RAG status to what is happening on the ground.

COG 04

Governance

Is accountability real or performative? We examine whether decision-making forums are empowered to act, or just venues for reporting what leadership wants to hear.

COG 05

Adoption

Will anyone actually use what you're building? Business readiness, change management, training - we test whether the organisation is being carried with the programme or left behind.

COG 06

Benefit Realisation

Can you demonstrate the value? We assess whether benefits are defined, owned, tracked, and - critically - whether anyone will still be accountable for them after the programme closes.

The Process

Five Steps. Eight Weeks.

A structured, time-bounded engagement that respects your team's capacity whilst still getting to the truth.

01

Pre-Discovery Questionnaire

You complete a structured briefing document. We arrive informed, not blank-slate. Your team's time is not wasted on orientation.

02

Discovery Call

Scoping session with the commissioning stakeholder. We agree scope, access, boundaries, and what a successful outcome looks like.

03

Active Fieldwork

Interviews, documentation review, and observation across all six cogs. We go wherever the evidence leads, and we read what the RAG reports don't say.

04

Authority Asset

A scored, evidenced PDF report across all six dimensions. Designed to be placed in front of a board or audit committee without further translation.

05

Stakeholder Debrief

A structured readout with the commissioning stakeholder and - where appropriate - the wider leadership team. We stay in the room for the hard questions.

Evidenced Delivery

Getting To The Root Cause

Three examples of programmes where independent scrutiny changed the trajectory.

Leeds Building Society

Core Banking & Mortgage Platform

Eight weeks behind schedule on arrival. The programme had stalled in the dependency between waterfall governance and an agile delivery team neither side understood. We diagnosed the fault line, rebuilt the coordination model, and transitioned the programme mid-flight.

Delivered on time - first among all suppliers

Betfred / Sharp Gaming

Core Digital Platform Replacement

3.5 million customer accounts to migrate. The programme lacked a dependency model robust enough to sequence the work safely. We built the governance framework, closed the dependency gaps, and managed the migration to completion with lower-than-projected service disruption.

3.5m accounts migrated successfully

The Co-operative Bank

Regulatory Redress Programme

250,000 mortgage accounts. A £100m regulatory programme running against a fixed regulator deadline. We identified a revised delivery approach that cut six months from the original timeline without compromising the regulatory standard - and won the argument in front of the board.

6 months saved. Regulatory standard maintained.
